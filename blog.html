How Do You Measure an A.I. Boom?

By Kevin Roose • Published 3 days ago

A chart created by METR, a nonprofit A.I. organization, has become an industrywide obsession as it measures the rapid development of big A.I. systems.

For years, the artificial intelligence industry has been obsessed with a single question: How do we know if we’re actually making progress? As models become more powerful, the old benchmarks are falling by the wayside. Now, a new metric created by the nonprofit organization METR is gaining traction as the de facto standard for measuring the rapid development of frontier AI systems.

The chart, which plots “effective compute” against performance on real-world tasks, has become a fixture in boardroom presentations and academic papers. It provides a clearer picture than raw parameter counts or FLOPs ever could.

The METR Methodology

METR’s approach focuses on “agentic” capabilities—can the AI complete complex, multi-step tasks without constant human intervention? This involves testing on software engineering, scientific research, and data analysis benchmarks.

Implications for the Future

Policymakers are watching closely. If these trends continue, the timeline for transformative AI may be shorter than many expect, necessitating urgent conversations about safety and alignment.
